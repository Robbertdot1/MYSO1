Pakistan Train Hijack Ends in Deadly Standoff, Security Forces Eliminate Insurgents

A hostage crisis on the Jaffar Express train in Balochistan has ended after security forces launched an operation against Baloch Liberation Army (BLA) insurgents. The attack resulted in 21 civilian deaths and four security personnel casualties. All 33 insurgents were neutralized, and the remaining hostages were rescued. The BLA had demanded the release of Baloch political prisoners during the standoff. Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif condemned the attack and met with survivors and rescue forces.

Pakistan strikes terrorist bases in Afghanistan after deadly border attack.

In response to a deadly assault on a Pakistani border outpost in South Waziristan that resulted in the deaths of 16 paramilitary soldiers, the Pakistan Air Force conducted targeted airstrikes on December 21, 2024, against terrorist bases in Afghanistan. The attack on the outpost was claimed by the Afghanistan-based Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan (TTP). Pakistan's Defense Minister, Khawaja Asif, reiterated the nation's commitment to counterterrorism efforts, emphasizing that Pakistan will continue to target militant sanctuaries beyond its borders to ensure national security.

Pakistan PM Vows to Combat Terrorism Amid Rising Militancy and Attacks.

Amid rising militant attacks, including the BLA’s deadly Jaffar Express hijacking in Balochistan, PM Shehbaz Sharif reaffirmed Pakistan’s commitment to fighting terrorism. The attack killed 21 civilians and four security personnel before forces neutralized all 33 insurgents and rescued hostages. Sharif condemned the assault, praised security forces, and vowed to eliminate terrorism. The BLA, demanding regional autonomy, has intensified its activities, underscoring Balochistan’s ongoing security challenges.
